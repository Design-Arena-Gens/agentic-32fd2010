Follow the hands-on guide to assemble AND and OR gate circuits in Proteus 8, validate their truth tables, and understand their real-world wiring.

Start Building

Project Overview

You will build a Proteus 8 project with digital switches feeding both AND and OR logic gates, driving LED indicators. The design uses standard TTL components, and the guide below walks through the schematic layout, wiring, and simulation steps.

Proteus 8.12+ TTL Logic Beginner Friendly

Required Components

  • 2× Digital Switch (from Virtual Instruments)
  • 1× Logic AND Gate (e.g., 74LS08)
  • 1× Logic OR Gate (e.g., 74LS32)
  • 2× LEDs (choose contrasting colors)
  • 2× 330Ω resistors (current limiting)
  • 1× Ground terminal
  • 1× +5V Power rail

Proteus Schematic Layout

  1. Open Proteus and start a new Schematic Capture project.
  2. Select components above via P (Pick Devices).
  3. Place the AND gate and OR gate horizontally with space for wiring.
  4. Add two switches on the left, label them A and B.
  5. Wire switches to both gate inputs using tidy, right-angle tracks.
  6. Attach LED + resistor pairs to the output of each gate.
  7. Connect LED cathodes to GND and feed their anodes through resistors.
  8. Bind both switches to +5V via pull-up wiring and defaults set to OFF.

Simulation Checklist

Double-click each switch to toggle logic levels during simulation. The AND gate LED should only light when both switches are high, whereas the OR gate LED lights when either switch is high. Confirm behavior with the interactive truth-table below.

Power & Signal Integrity Tips

  • Use a single VCC rail labelled VCC and fan it out to the ICs.
  • Keep input traces short and avoid crossings to maintain clarity.
  • Enable Snap to Grid for clean alignment and easier debugging.
  • Color-code power (red) and ground (blue/black) for quick visuals.
  • Add labels such as AND_OUT and OR_OUT to track signals.

Export & Documentation

Once verified, export the schematic as PDF via Output > Export Graphics. Include annotated truth tables and simulation screenshots for lab reports or coursework submissions.

For PCB follow-up, switch to PCB Layout, import the netlist, and map each gate to its footprint (e.g., DIP-14 package).
